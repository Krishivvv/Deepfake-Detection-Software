The deployed model is a frame-level ResNet-50 CNN fine-tuned for binary deepfake classification on a subset of FaceForensics++. For each uploaded video the app extracts {{ num_frames }} face crops, runs each through the CNN, and uses the mean of per-frame fake probabilities as the video-level score.

Training pipeline

  1. Frame extraction (OpenCV, 32 evenly-spaced frames per video)
  2. Face detection & cropping (facenet-pytorch MTCNN, 224×224 with 20-px margin)
  3. CNN training (train_cnn.py) with ImageNet-pretrained backbone, last block trainable
  4. Threshold calibration (tune_cnn_threshold.py) — sweep on val, pick threshold that maximises macro-F1

Limitations

  • Trained on a small subset (700 train / 150 val / 150 test videos).
  • 4:1 fake:real class skew. Macro-F1 is reported alongside accuracy so majority-class collapse cannot hide poor performance on real videos.
  • Real recall is ~42 % at the deployed threshold — the model still mislabels a non-trivial fraction of real videos as fake.
  • Research demo. Not a forensic-grade detector.
